Pilots

Navigate the plane, ensuring their aircraft is below its weight limit and balanced, checking the fuel supply and weather conditions, submitting flight plans to air traffic control and communicate with them through the aircrafts radio system. $100,000 per year and requires a bachelor's degree of aircraft operations, aviation, aeronautical engineering, etc.

Air Traffic Controller

Monitor and directing the movement of aircraft on the ground and in the air, controlling all ground traffic at airport runways and taxiways, issuing landing and takeoff instructions to pilots. $60,000 per year and requires an associate's degree in navigation.

Flight Attendant 

Keeping passengers safe, ensuring that everyone follows security regulations and that the flight deck is secure, try to make flights comfortable and stress free for passengers, may have to deal with passengers who display disruptive behavior. $50,000 per year and requires a high school diploma and many years in customer service.

Management

Responsible for discussing with the airline personnel who oversees design certification, installation, test and turn up, maintenance of equipments and other activities involved in the airline's maintenance department. $100,000 per year and requires an associates degree in business.

Engineer

Responsible for ensuring that all components of the plane are in proper working order, making any of the repairs if a mechanical issue does arise, interpreting complicated flight-related gauges and instruments, helping pilots with navigation. $70,000 per year and requires a bachelor's degree in aviation.

    Assistance

    Alas Airlines always wishes to make your flight easier and secure. We offer special assistance to those who specifically need extra/special assistance.

    Accessibility 
  • If you have special needs that require equipment you may request early boarding
  • We can also help those with special needs to board and exit the plane, and can help with most special assistance that may be required
  • For those who need hearing assistance, they can ask attendance for updates on important announcement and there will be captioning available for safety videos
  • For those who need vision assistance you should request a courtesy wheelchair, or ask one of the ticket agents for help getting to your gate.
  • In order to have a service animal you must be able to provide medical/mental health documents, documents confirming vaccination of animals, and documents to prove that the animals have been trained to assist you
